Chapter I – Logaros, the Copper Kettle Inn
In the land of Eidoria, where forests stretch for leagues and villages glow with lantern light, tales are still told of Archivon, the lost City of Knowledge. Archivon was said to be a place where all truths were gathered, where wisdom shone brighter than gold. Yet the city vanished, slipping into legend, leaving behind only whispers of its wonders.
In the quiet valley village of Logaros, life passes with the steady rhythm of inns, post houses, and travelers crossing its river bridge. But on this night, the townsfolk gathered in the Copper Kettle Inn, eager to witness the words of a wandering scholar: Master Aldorren.
The inn grew hushed as the flamboyant mage strode onto the small stage, a staff in hand, its crystal vial glowing green. He lifted his arms wide and spoke:
“Good folk of Logaros! Travelers, villagers, seekers of truth — lend me your ears! For tonight, you are most fortunate indeed… to hear the words of Aldorren the Learned, master of curatives, healer of wounds, scourge of disease, and — dare I say it — custodian of forgotten wisdom!”
Gasps rose as he shook the vial, fizzing green, before continuing with a flourish:
“I have wandered forests deep and mountains high, crossed rivers wide and caverns dark, all in pursuit of knowledge. And tonight, I bring you a secret — the very elixir of life! With a single draught, wounds close, fevers vanish, pains melt away. A gift not from superstition, no, but from the careful study of ancient texts!”
He waved a battered old book above his head, as if it were priceless.
“Do not mistake me for a hedge-healer, brewing charms in a cauldron by guesswork. Oh no! I, Aldorren, have studied the works of Archivon itself — the lost City of Knowledge! Where others saw legends, I saw blueprints! Where others saw fairy tales, I saw formulas! Yes, my friends — Archivon was real! Its secrets lie scattered, waiting for minds sharp enough, bold enough, worthy enough… to grasp them!
But beware! Archivon’s wisdom is not for the faint-hearted. Only those with the courage to follow the path of truth — through danger, doubt, and deception — may yet find the academy’s gates once more. And I, Aldorren, will guide those brave enough to walk beside me.”
The inn erupted in applause and murmurs, though not all were convinced. Among those listening were Pip and Luna, each came for their own reasons, both curious but cautious. In conversation with him afterward, they found Aldorren’s answers vague, his knowledge oddly shallow for one who claimed so much. Whispers of a mentor, Seraphius, slipped from his tongue — a greater master who had cast Aldorren aside for greed.
Suspicion weighed against hope. Yet when Aldorren announced he would travel next to the larger town of Fenkrey for another lecture, Pip and Luna chose to follow. Perhaps he was a fraud, but perhaps his stories would still lead them closer to the truth of Archivon.
And so, leaving the valley village behind, the road stretched before them, deeper into the heart of Eidoria, toward Fenkrey and whatever awaited them there.
